PHASE 1: CONSTRUCTION AND RECLASSIFICATION

Completed in 2010 & 2011

Phase 1 of the Firestone Renovation Project focused on two major construction efforts: the construction of new Rare Books & Special Collections spaces on C-Floor, and construction of an extension in the Northwest Stairwell to the 2nd and 3rd floors of Firestone Library. This work was completed in early November 2011.

Phase 1 also included a project to make it easier to find books in the stacks, which was a major goal of the Firestone Library master renovation plan. The Richardson Reclassification Project re-classified all of the material in the open Firestone stacks and in the study rooms from the Richardson call number system to the more-widely used Library of Congress call number system. As the Richardson material was reclassified, it was integrated with the Library of Congress material to create a single call number sequence for Firestone Library. Simultaneously, the overall collection organization was simplified by combining related collections (e.g., Arab, SY, SYS) to reduce the number of separate stack sequences. The reclassification is complete, and signage has been displayed throughout the library indicating collection locations, as well as additional staff ‘book finders’ on C Floor to provide assistance to users.
